Quantum Error Correction

A blog series by Boaz Micah 9 Articles

Quantum computers have the potential to revolutionise computing — but quantum information is extraordinarily fragile. This series takes you from the fundamentals of classical error correction all the way through to simulating modern quantum error correction codes, building understanding from the ground up.

We begin with the intuition from classical coding theory, identify the unique obstacles that quantum mechanics imposes, and then develop the tools — entanglement, stabiliser measurements, the Pauli group — needed to overcome them. By the end, you will understand Shor's 9-qubit code, why it works, and how to simulate it numerically using state-of-the-art tools.
