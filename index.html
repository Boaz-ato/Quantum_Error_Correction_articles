Quantum Error Correction

Boaz Micah

Quantum computers have the potential to revolutionise computing but quantum information is very fragile. We need a way to protect the quantum information. Our current best approach to solving this is quantum error correction, where we protect the information in a qubit by using mutiple physical qubits. In this series, we will understand the process used to protect quantum information and how to simulate it. We will start from classical error correction, understand why quantum error correction is different and go through the different approaches used to protect quantum information. The goal is to build intuition as to how the whole process works.

By the end, you will both understand and be able to simulate differnet quantum error corrections protocols and identify the threshold below which they become useful.
