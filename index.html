Quantum Error Correction

A blog series by Boaz Micah 9 Articles

Quantum computers have the potential to revolutionise computing but quantum information is very fragile. We need a way to protect the quantum information. Our current best approach to solving this is quantum error correction. This series takes you from the fundamentals of classical error correction all the way through to simulating quantum error correction codes, building understanding from the ground up.

We begin with the intuition from classical coding theory, identify the unique obstacles that quantum mechanics imposes, and then develop the tools needed to overcome them. By the end, you will both understand and be able to simulate differnet quantum error corrections protocols and identify the threshold below which they become useful.
